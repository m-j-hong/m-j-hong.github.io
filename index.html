About

design isn’t made. it’s discovered. it isn’t created. it’s remembered
i think design is one of those beautiful things that directly reminds us of the truth of it all. it’s impossible to fully describe because it’s one step in our world of “separate” forms and one step in the boundless, indivisible love of the cosmos.

good design is something that you know exactly what to do with before you even see it; everything about its construction is already identical to the purpose that it serves. it’s beautiful to look at, but not because it makes you think. it allows you to stop thinking, to enter into doing directly from being. interacting with it is a dance, where you respond to it and it responds to you seamlessly. there is no delay, no lag of thought-computing between intent and action. design is empathetic, it listens to you. the best design is something that you feel like already completely understands you as a user. the most effective design comes from the closest, most sensitive and receptive listening.

our job as designers is to get out of the way. we must become porous enough that solutions arise through us. we are not creators, but conduits; we are thresholds through which what already exists can come into form

nature is the most perfect designer; natural selection IS design. not forcing something into a space, but rather allowing the shaping and molding of form by the patterns, needs, behaviors of the immediate environment.

in order to allow good design to happen, you have to put yourself in a profoundly empathetic position. design is not something you think about. it shows up in how you feel, but deeper than that, it is dictated by the patterns by which you live. design is not a thing, not an object, but rather a doorway. a threshold between the manifested and the manifesting. bad design is full of friction, resistance. it is insulation between what is physically perceivable and what is coming into being. good design is smooth, fluid, elegant in all patterns of actions it responds to.
